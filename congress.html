Congressional Net Worth is Too Damn High

Let's dive headfirst into the 1%ers cash-filled pools. While the average American family is making $59,039 annually, most of our faithful congressional representatives are netting millions. These elected officials are the voice of the American people and are responsible for making laws that influence our daily lives, but how can they understand the strife of the American family when they earn 12 times the average household income?!

Wealthiest Congressional Representatives' Net Worth

Data acquired from Roll Call

The map above displays the wealthiest congressional representative of each state. The industries in which they built their wealth range from real estate, mining to technology. The greener the state, the more millions the representative has. Net worth is calculated based on investments, real estate, trusts, and bank accounts. There is no state whose richest representative is not a millionaire.

#1 Darrell Issa

With a whopping $283.3 million net worth, Representative Darrell Issa of California is the richest member of Congress. He is a Republican who made his riches in the 1990s while leading Directed Electronics Inc., a Vista-based manufacturer of vehicle antitheft devices that he created. Darrell has been an open Trump supporter, earning him multiple anti-Trump protests at his district office. Because he pitied us poor peons, he declared he would not be running for re-election. A surprising but not close second, Montana Representative Greg Gianforte's current net worth is estimated at $135.7 million. In Conclusion

Wealth in Congress has risen to exorbitant levels, with many congressional representatives belonging to the 1%. This creates an awkward situation for the rest of us trying to just pursue a life debt free while being beaten down by our student loan debt. Our wealthiest congressional representatives don't know how to not make money and they bring that money lust to Washington, but nothing is trickling down. We constantly hear jobs, jobs, jobs but those newly created jobs continue to exploit lower income earners while lining the pockets of our faithful 1%ers. The sad truth is that there is not much we can do to change this without a constitutional amendment to make elections publicly funded in order to eliminate the corrupting influence of Big Money campaign contributions. Unless we make this change, the monied special interests will continue to spend millions to elect millionaire candidates who will do their bidding, while ignoring or simply paying lip service to the people they are supposed to be representing.
