Are All Politics Local?

About the Work
The North Carolina General Assembly has had multiple systematic attempts to redraw for majority-party advantage, including using racial gerrymandering as a tactic. This work follows redistricting of congressional districts after the 1990, 2000, and 2010 United States Decennial Census and looks at the competitiveness of each district in election years. My hypothesis is that if the North Carolina General Assembly successfully gerrymandered districts to maintain party majority, there would be a lack of competition in historically Republican/Democratic Party represented districts as well as low donation totals. There would be high competition in districts over open seats which would be identified by high campaign donation totals for all candidates. Congressional campaign donations, specifically total receipts per district candidate and total receipts by all party-affilitiated candidates, was acquired from the Federal Election Commission campaign finance online data portal. Only the Republican and Democratic party candidates were chosen for the general elections in two year increments from 2000 to 2016.

Suggested Future Work
Studies have documented that incumbents serving on powerful congressional committees raise more funds during congressional campaigns. An interesting avenue to advance this work would analyze any changes in the patterns of giving when legislators switch committees. This analysis would investigate the influence campaign contributions suggest from PACs on influencing legislative outcomes.

Software
QGIS 3.6 Noosa’s plug-in cartogram3, created by Christopher Fink, was used to create anamorphic cartograms. Esri ArcPro 10.6 was used to edit, manipulate (via tools), and display data to create thematic maps. The thematic maps, bivariate and cartogram, were exported to ArcGIS online. ArcGIS online was used to create a Story Map from the exported webmap. D3.js was used to create interactive graphs adapted from FlowingData’s “Animated Square Pie Tutorial” by Nathan Yau.
