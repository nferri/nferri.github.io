Sacramento Floodplains Map

Sacramento, California is dependent on levees to prevent major flooding. The system of levees were built during the first settlements in the late 1800s and have improvements to the levees have been instrumental in protecting the residents of Sacramento. Levee systems checks are an on-going initiative, with improvements happening to the highest need levees first. If any of the levee systems were to fail, areas of Sacramento County and Sacramento City would experience flooding and high cost damages.

The map below shows Sacramento County and the City of Sacramento's housing units and population from the US Census. Areas where the color is the deepest are high-population/housing areas. The levee systems are indicated by red lines and the 100 year floodplain is indicated in red. The layers are able to be turned on and off to experience the data without crowding the map.

Data acquired from City of Sacramento, Sacramento County, and the US Census Bureau
